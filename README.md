# Scalable and Overcompression-Resistant Graph Neural Networks: Improvements and Performance Enhancements
Graph Neural Networks (GNNs), as the core technology for processing non-Euclidean topological data, have been widely applied in social networks, bioinformatics, recommendation systems and other fields. However, two major bottlenecks, namely insufficient scalability in real-world large-scale graph data and feature overcompression caused by multi-layer message passing, have severely restricted the efficiency and generalization performance of such models. Taking these issues as the entry point, this thesis focuses on the systematic improvement and performance enhancement of scalable and overcompression-resistant graph neural networks. By analyzing the defects of existing mechanisms including sampling strategies, message aggregation and receptive field regulation, this study proposes optimization schemes from the dimensions of architecture design, propagation rules and complexity control. Furthermore, it verifies the comprehensive advantages of the proposed model in large-scale graph inference, information fidelity, task accuracy and other aspects through theoretical derivation and multi-scenario benchmark experiments. This research aims to break the inherent trade-off between efficiency and expressive power, provide theoretical support and practical solutions for efficient and robust graph representation learning, and promote the deployment and application of graph neural networks in complex real-world scenarios.
